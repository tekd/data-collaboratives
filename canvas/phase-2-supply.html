Phase 2: Supply

Once the demand side of the equation is well understood and articulated, the supply of data and expertise (i.e., human capital) should be explored in order to determine how the supply can address the demand (or if there is not a match between supply and demand and, therefore, a data collaborative would unconducive to the issue at hand).

Step 3: Expertise: Data Science and Organizational Competency

Discussion

Cross-sector data sharing requires not only the bringing together or diverse datasets, but also collaboration between people and organizations with different skills and institutional norms. An upfront understanding of how human and institutional capacity and cultures either do or do not mesh can help to define optimal roles and responsibilities and identify capacity gaps that need to be filled (through additional partnerships or other mechanisms).

Questions and Considerations

  • Internal

    1. Evaluate and review internal capacity
    2. Build high-level internal support
    3. Identify internal data literacy and human capital
    4. Collect and share examples of how other organizations have leveraged data

  • External

    1. Which entities could provide additional competencies (data science and otherwise)?

Step 4: Data Supply

Discussion

In many ways, data collaboratives are means for filling gaps in existing institutional data supplies. In order to fill such gaps, organizers should conduct an upfront audit of both the existing internal data supply and the potential supply of data existing in other sectors. Such an audit can help inform and prioritize outreach to external data holders and ensure that newly accessible datasets are well-positioned for filling the most important data gaps.

Questions and Considerations

  • Internal: Conduct due diligence research and a data audit
    1. What data currently exists that will be useful in addressing the problem?
    2. What are the data gaps that slow progress toward addressing the problem?
  • External: Map the potential supply side based on identified gaps
    1. What data do you already have (develop a data inventory)?
    2. Which entities (whether private, governmental or nongovernmental) possess and could provide the needed data?
